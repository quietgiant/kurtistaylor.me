about me

I was born in 1995 in Kalamazoo, Michigan and moved to Fort Wayne, Indiana in 1997 where I have lived ever since. Growing up, I played basketball and baseball. I also loved to play all sorts of video games, just like I do now. One of my favorites was Pokemon Blue, which I played on my now-ancient Gameboy Color. One day, my friend showed me the "old man glitch" and I had my first encounter with the most notorious Pokemon from that series - Missingno. Curious, I stumbled into a world of "bits" and "variables" and "pointers" when searching for answers online. I was in the world of programming for the first time.


Ever since, I have spent much of my time learning about computers and electronics. I built my first computer as a young teenager, where I installed Ubuntu and got my first taste of Linux. In high school, I learned my first programming language, Java... although now-a-days I prefer C#. I have developed a passion for problem solving techniques and understand how technology can interact with it.


In 2014, I began studying Computer Science at Purdue University Fort Wayne (PFW). I decided to pursue a graduate degree and began studying Applied Computer Science at PFW in 2018. I graduated with a Masters of Science in Applied Computer Science in 2020.


Computer science offers a wide variety of disciplines; I am mostly interested in web applications (especially progressive), computational finance tools, algorithmic trading bots, and distributed systems. In general, I am interested in the ways we can apply technology to contemporary problems in order to solve them.
